Pipo's Paneer

Quick Paneer Curry

Ingredients

For Paneer Marination:

  • 350g paneer, cubed
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon (kashmiri) chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 teaspoons oil, for roasting

For Curry:

  • 1 tablespoon ghee or butter
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 black cardamom pod (optional)
  • 3 cm cinnamon stick
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon (Kashmiri) chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 350 ml tomato puree or passata or chopped tomatoes
  • 100 ml plain or Greek yogurt (more heat-resistant)
  • 200 ml water (adjust based on desired consistency)
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander, finely chopped (optional)

Marination and Paneer Preparation

  1. In a large bowl, mix turmeric, chili powder, ginger garlic paste, and salt with the paneer cubes.
  2. Let the paneer marinate for 30 minutes.
  3. Heat 3 teaspoons of oil in a non-stick frying pan and shallow fry the marinated paneer cubes until golden brown on the outside. Remove and set aside.

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan or deep frying pan, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee and 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, black cardamom, cinnamon stick, green cardamom, and cloves. Sauté for 1 minute until aromatic.
  3. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Stir in the ginger garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Lower the heat and add turmeric powder, chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Add the tomato puree and mix well. Cover and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oil starts separating from the mixture.
  7. Lower the heat and add the yogurt, stirring continuously to prevent curdling. Cook until well combined.
  8. Add 1 cup of water and bring to a gentle simmer. Adjust the consistency as needed.
  9. Add the fried paneer cubes and gently mix. Cover and let it simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse.
  10. Sprinkle crushed kasuri methi and garam masala, then garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot with roti, naan, or steamed rice.
